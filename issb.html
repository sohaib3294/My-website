Information about ISSB Tests

Your complete ISSB preparation snapshot from screening to conference.

What is ISSB?

ISSB (Inter Services Selection Board) is the final selection process for candidates applying to Pakistan Army, Navy, and Air Force officer entries. It evaluates intelligence, personality, leadership, communication, confidence, and physical fitness.

Initial Computer Test (Before ISSB)

Before ISSB, candidates usually pass an initial computer-based test. This test is commonly divided into three parts:

  • Verbal Intelligence: Analogy, series, word relation, and logic-based questions.
  • Non-Verbal Intelligence: Pattern recognition, figures, sequences, and visual reasoning.
  • Academic MCQs: Subject-based multiple-choice questions (English, Math, General Knowledge, and related basics).

Main ISSB Test Stages

  • 1) Reporting & Documentation: Document checking, registration, and basic instructions.
  • 2) Intelligence Tests: Verbal and non-verbal IQ tests under time pressure.
  • 3) Psychological Tests: Word association, sentence completion, picture story writing, and self-description.
  • 4) GTO Tasks: Group discussions, command task, progressive group tasks, lecturette, and planning exercises.
  • 5) Personal Interview: One-to-one interview focused on personality, background, motivation, and general awareness.
  • 6) Conference: Final board decision by psychologists, GTO, and interview panel.

Common Preparation Areas

  • Current affairs and Pakistan studies basics
  • Clear communication in Urdu/English
  • Confidence, honesty, and positive body language
  • Daily physical routine (running, push-ups, stamina)
  • Leadership behavior in team tasks
  • Time management in written/psych tests

Documents to Keep Ready

  • Original CNIC / B-Form and photocopies
  • Academic certificates and marks sheets
  • Passport-size photographs
  • Call letter / roll number slip
  • Any required domicile/character documents

Important Tips

  • Be natural and truthful in all responses.
  • Avoid memorized/artificial answers.
  • Participate actively in group tasks without dominating.
  • Stay disciplined, polite, and energetic throughout.
  • Sleep well before test days and arrive on time.
